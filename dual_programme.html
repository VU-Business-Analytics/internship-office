Dual programme Business Analytics

The dual Master's programme combines work and study. During this programme the student is employed part time, and studies part time. The work has to be relevant for the study. In addition, the student gets 12 credit points (cp) for the work time. The external graduation project Master Project Business Analytics can also be carried out at the same company. Most dual students choose this option.

Admission to the dual master's programme is granted to those who have a Business Analytics Bachelor's degree. For those with another university Bachelor's degree such as Mathematics, Econometrics, Computer Science, or a Bachelor's degree from an institute of higher education, admission may be granted on an individual basis. You can contact Joost Berkhout about admissions. Those seeking admission to the dual master's programme should realise that admission also depends on obtaining suitable employment. 

If you have found a nice dual job position, you can send the Dual Work Period proposal to Annemieke van Goor, preferably before June 1, 2022. If you need the course manual and/or the Dual Work Period proposal, please send a request to Annemieke van Goor.

If you have any questions regarding the course Dual Work Period you can contact the Dual Work period coordinator Annemieke van Goor, and if you have questions about the dual master Business Analytics the master coordinatorEduard Belitser.
